Publications

HiDe

arXiv Project

ICML 2026 HiDe: Rethinking The Zoom-IN Method in High Resolution MLLMs via Hierarchical Decoupling

Xianjie Liu, Yiman Hu, Yixiong Zou, et al.

Addressing the poor performance of MLLMs on high-resolution images, previous work attributed this to insufficient perception and adopted "zoom-in" strategies. Through hierarchical decoupling analysis, we discovered the real bottleneck is background interference rather than object size. We propose HiDe, a training-free framework that uses Token-level Attention Decoupling (TAD) to locate key regions and Layout Preservation Decoupling (LPD) to build compact representations. HiDe achieves SOTA on V*Bench (Qwen2.5-VL 7B: 92.1), surpassing RL methods with 75% memory reduction.

E-VAds

arXiv GitHub

ICML 2026 E-VAds: An E-commerce Short Videos Understanding Benchmark for MLLMs

Xianjie Liu, Yiman Hu, Liang Wu, et al.

Addressing the lack of dedicated benchmarks for e-commerce short videos and existing models' difficulty in handling open commercial intent reasoning, we propose E-VAds, the first e-commerce short video understanding benchmark. It contains 3,961 high-quality videos and 19,785 open-ended QA pairs, covering five tasks across perception and reasoning dimensions: basic perception, cross-modal detection, marketing logic, consumer insight, and compliance review. We design a multi-agent annotation system and propose MG-GRPO multi-granularity reward strategy. E-VAds-R1 achieves 109.2% performance improvement in commercial intent reasoning with only hundreds of samples.

PDFNet

arXiv Project

CVPR 2026 High-Precision Dichotomous Image Segmentation via Depth Integrity-Prior and Fine-Grained Patch Strategy

Xianjie Liu, Keren Fu, Qijun Zhao

Addressing the dilemma of insufficient precision in non-diffusion methods and expensive computation in diffusion methods for high-resolution dichotomous image segmentation (DIS), this paper introduces monocular pseudo-depth maps as structural priors. We discover that foreground depth variance is significantly lower than background (Depth Integrity-Prior), and design a multimodal fusion architecture with Depth Integrity-Prior Loss to enhance structural perception. The proposed PDFNet achieves SOTA on DIS-5K with less than 50% parameters and approximately 5× faster inference compared to diffusion models.

DIS-SAM

arXiv GitHub

ICME 2025 Promoting Segment Anything Model towards Highly Accurate Dichotomous Image Segmentation

Xianjie Liu, Keren Fu, Yao Jiang, Qijun Zhao

The Segment Anything Model (SAM) represents a significant breakthrough in foundation models for computer vision. However, its segmentation masks lack fine-grained details, particularly in accurately delineating object boundaries. We propose DIS-SAM, a two-stage framework that integrates SAM with IS-Net to achieve highly accurate dichotomous image segmentation while maintaining SAM's promptable design. We introduce parameter orthogonalization and GT data augmentation strategies to enhance generalization. DIS-SAM significantly advances SAM, HQ-SAM, and Pi-SAM by ~8.5%, ~6.9%, and ~3.7% maximum F-measure on DIS-5K dataset.
